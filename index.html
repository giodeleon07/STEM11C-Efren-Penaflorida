ABOUT
RISING FROM THE SLUMS: The Story of a Hero
Written by Abraham Filomeno and Isabelle Mangilin
Revised by Sharmaine Trellier


In the heart of Cavite City, amidst the sprawling slums and the heaps of garbage, a hero was born. Efren Peñaflorida grew up in the stark contrast of life's unforgiving realities. His playground was a dumpsite; his toys were the discarded remnants of a society that knew nothing of his existence. His father, a humble tricycle driver, and his mother, a dedicated housewife, strived to make ends meet. They supplemented their income by venturing into small businesses, selling noodles and fish crackers.

Young Efren spent his childhood days traversing from Baclaran to Cavite, selling fish crackers. He considered it a normal part of life until he encountered children from different social statuses. The sneers, the disgust, and the mockery were a revelation–his life was far from ordinary. He saw his reflection in the mirror of societal disparities, and it was a harsh awakening.

Despite his circumstances, Efren held a deep-seated love for education. Scholarships from different organizations fueled his academic journey. He completed his primary and secondary education, then pursued a Computer Technology degree at San Sebastian College, graduating with the highest honors. Not stopping there, he went on to earn a degree in Secondary Education at Cavite State University, finishing cum laude. However, the journey was not without its trials. There were times when Efren, burdened by poverty, felt the urge to quit education. He was a victim of relentless bullying, both verbal and physical, further amplifying his sense of inferiority. Balancing school life with a demanding personal life was a constant struggle. He vividly recalls a terrifying encounter with a group of gangsters who pelted him with stones, a harrowing experience that left him questioning the worth of his educational pursuit.

Nevertheless, Efren was blessed with a strong support system. His sponsorship by World Vision International, an organization dedicated to helping people experiencing poverty, played a pivotal role in his life. His mother's unwavering faith in the power of education and her relentless optimism were his guiding light. Another source of inspiration was Harnin Manalaysay, fondly known as KB!, a pastor at Club 8586. When vengeance against the bullies clouded Efren's mind, and the allure of joining a gang seemed tempting, it was KB! who steered him away, reminding him of the grim outcomes–jail or a cemetery.

Efren Peñaflorida's life is a testament to the transformative power of education and the resilience of the human spirit. From the slums of Cavite City to becoming a beacon of hope, his journey is an inspiring tale of determination, resilience, and courage.
